# SDET Orchestration Layer - Building Guide

## Your Goal
Build a **personal testing framework** that orchestrates your existing tools and patterns across projects. Not a market product—a genuine productivity multiplier + portfolio piece.

---

## Core Principles While Building

**1. Solve Your Actual Problem First**
- Focus on YOUR workflow, not hypothetical users
- Authenticity > novelty
- Make it portable from day one (even if just for you)

**2. Engineer It Properly**
- Write it like someone else might use it (good practice, easier GitHub release)
- Modular, clean code
- Document architectural decisions, not just features

**3. Capture the "Why"**
- As you build, note WHY you made specific orchestration choices
- This becomes your blog post / resume gold later
- Shows engineering judgment, not just tool-stacking

---

## Building Checklist

- [ ] **Identify the core orchestration**: What tools/steps are you currently repeating?
- [ ] **Map your workflow**: How do they chain together in your projects?
- [ ] **Build modularly**: Each component should be independent, but callable together
- [ ] **Make it portable**: Config/setup should be minimal for new repos
- [ ] **Document decisions**: In comments or a separate doc—why this approach?
- [ ] **Test it across your personal projects**: If it works on 3+ different repos, it works
- [ ] **Clean and refactor**: Once it works, engineer it for clarity

## For Your Resume (Later)

**Don't call it:**
- A "plugin"
- A "novel testing tool"
- Something that "solves a market problem"

**Do call it:**
- "Testing Orchestration Layer"
- "Personal SDET Framework"
